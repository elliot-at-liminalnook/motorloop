# Leg-weapon battlebot — actuator spec (first cut)

Sizing the **M1 vertical retract** (the dodge motion, and the spear axis) for the
leg-weapon design ([`motorloop-battlebot-design`](../) memory). Kinetic spear —
the induction heater is parked pending EO approval (SPARC §14.1 EMF/electrical).
Generated by `sim/scripts/gen_battlebot_actuator_spec.py` (reuses the profiled
motors in `sim/tests/motors.py`); figure `figures/battlebot/retract_power.png`.

**Method.** Lift the leg `LIFT_M = 0.20 m` (~8 in) in a dodge-time budget, triangular
accel/decel, moving mass `1.5 kg` (assumed — refine from CAD). Peak force
`F = m(a+g)`, peak shaft power `P = F·v / η_belt`. **Power is the feasibility gate**:
a belt ratio trades torque↔speed freely, but `F·v ≤ P_motor` always. Motor ceiling
= max mechanical power at the optimal point, `P = V²/(4·R_ll)` (back-EMF = V/2).

## What the dodge costs (1.5 kg, 0.20 m)

| dodge time | peak speed | peak accel | peak force | shaft power |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 0.10 s | 4.0 m/s | 8.2 g | 135 N | **634 W** |
| 0.125 s | 3.2 m/s | 5.2 g | 92 N | **345 W** |
| 0.15 s | 2.7 m/s | 3.6 g | 68 N | **213 W** |
| 0.20 s | 2.0 m/s | 2.0 g | 45 N | **105 W** |
| 0.25 s | 1.6 m/s | 1.3 g | 34 N | **64 W** |

Power scales hard with speed (`P ∝ m·d²/t³`) — halving the dodge time ~8×'s the power.

## Motor ceilings + verdict

| motor (profiled) | ceiling @24 V | @48 V (rule max) | fastest dodge @24 V / @48 V |
|---|---|---|---|
| iPower GM2804 (gimbal) | 16 W | 64 W | none / 0.25 s — **out** |
| Nanotec DB42S03 | 96 W (4.5× I) | 384 W (8.9× I) | 0.25 s / 0.125 s |
| maxon EC 45 flat 50 W | 180 W (4.7× I) | 720 W (9.4× I) | 0.20 s / **0.10 s** |

## Conclusions

- **The gimbal is hopeless** (16 W) — confirms the RL hunch; it's a positioning
  motor, not a weapon motor.
- **DB42 / maxon class buys a *moderate* dodge (~0.20–0.25 s) at a safe 24 V.**
  That's borderline against the spinners we modelled (closing ~1.4–2.8 m/s → a
  ~0.15–0.25 s danger window). Good enough for a slow opponent, marginal for a fast
  one.
- **A *fast* dodge (≤0.125 s) needs either 48 V bursts or a bigger motor.** Voltage
  is a strong lever (`P ∝ V²`): 24→48 V quadruples available power and lets the
  maxon hit a 0.10 s dodge. But 48 V on a 12 V-rated motor is a **burst-only,
  accept-the-risk** combat practice (demag + insulation + 9× rated current). Cleaner:
  pick a motor *rated* for the bus and the burst — i.e. **a small outrunner / bigger
  BLDC than any of the three profiled here**, in the few-hundred-W-burst class.
- **The 2× walker weight bonus pays for that motor.** The honest sizing target is
  "a motor whose `V²/(4R)` clears your dodge-time power" — pick the dodge time first
  (from the spinner speed you must beat), then the motor follows.

## Caveats / next refinements

- Power-only first cut. Not yet checked: **reflected inertia** (the 5–13 g
  acceleration through motor+belt inertia), the **belt ratio/pulley** that lands the
  (F,v) in the motor's envelope, **thermal duty** for repeated dodges, and **spear
  penetration force** (may exceed the retract force).
- **Inverter must pass the burst current** (8–30 A) — that's our FOC/gate-driver
  domain; the drive, not just the motor, has to survive the dodge.
- Refine `MASS_KG` and the weight class, then re-run; the motor *class* won't move
  much, the exact part will.
